# French supporting documents — what's accepted and the rules

Grounding for advising on the **pièces justificatives** a démarche requires. This is
about *acceptability rules*, not reading a scan (see `../documents/`). Which exact
pièces a given démarche needs comes from its **official checklist** — do not invent a
requirement; here you explain the rules for the documents commonly requested.

Discipline: state a rule only if you're sure; for an amount or a list that may have
changed, say it depends and point the user to the official page rather than asserting a
stale figure. The official portal is **service-public.gouv.fr** (the old `service-public.fr`
now redirects there).

## Justificatif de domicile (proof of address)

- **Recency depends on the démarche — there is NO universal "3 months" rule.** Verified
  windows: **CNI & passeport → less than 1 year**; **carte grise / immatriculation → less
  than 6 months**; **inscription sur les listes électorales → less than 3 months**. Use the
  window the specific procedure states; never default to 3 months.
- **Accepted proofs** (CNI/passeport fiche): facture d'**électricité, gaz, eau**; **facture
  de téléphone — y compris mobile**; **avis d'imposition** or certificat de non-imposition;
  **attestation/facture d'assurance habitation**; **quittance de loyer** issued by an agency
  or organisme social (a handwritten quittance from a private landlord is **not** enough on
  its own); titre de propriété; **relevé CAF** mentioning the aides au logement.
- A **mobile-phone bill is accepted** — the old "fixed line / box only" restriction is
  obsolete.
- **Hébergé chez un tiers** (living at someone else's address): three documents together —
  an **attestation d'hébergement sur l'honneur** signed by the host, the **host's ID**, and a
  **justificatif de domicile in the host's name** recent in the window the démarche requires
  (e.g. < 1 year for a passport/CNI). A person living with parents or a friend uses this.
  - In the attestation the host states, on their honour, that they host the named person at
    their address **free of charge (à titre gratuit)**. It may be **temporary (à titre temporaire)**
    — there is **NO minimum-duration rule**; do NOT claim the host must have hosted them "> 3 months".
  - It carries: host's NOM/Prénom + address (+ phone), the hosted person's NOM/Prénom, the
    statement of hosting, and **Fait à … le …** with the host's signature. (Sample wording:
    "Je soussigné, <host>, demeurant au <address>, atteste sur l'honneur héberger à titre gratuit
    à mon domicile Monsieur/Madame <person>, à titre temporaire …".)
- **Logé à l'hôtel** (hotel accommodation): an **attestation/certificate from the hotel** plus the
  **invoice (facture) for the last month**.
- A **taxe d'habitation** or **avis d'imposition** in the applicant's name also serves as proof of address.

## Contrat d'engagement au respect des principes de la République (CERP)

- **Mandatory for EVERY titre de séjour request — first issue AND renewal** (since the Jan 2024
  immigration law, art. L412-7 CESEDA). The applicant signs a one-page Ministère de l'Intérieur form
  committing to 7 republican principles (liberté personnelle; expression et conscience; égalité
  femmes-hommes; dignité humaine; devise et symboles; intégrité territoriale; laïcité).
- **Refusing to sign ⇒ the préfet refuses the titre**; a later serious breach can have it withdrawn.
  Signed with place/date, NOM PRÉNOM, NUMÉRO ÉTRANGER, signature.
- Blank form to download (share the link when the user needs it):
  https://www.immigration.interieur.gouv.fr/limmigration-en-france/l-administration-numerique-pour-les-etrangers-en-France/le-contrat-d-engagement-a-respecter-les-principes-de-la-republique

## Pièce d'identité

- French nationals: **CNI** or **passeport**, valid — or sometimes expired by less than a
  set period for a renewal (only if the procedure allows it).
- Foreign nationals: valid **titre de séjour** + passport. (Which number is which — visa N°
  vs passport MRZ vs titre N° — see `../documents/documents-administratifs.md`.)
- Bring **originals** to an in-person RDV; online démarches take a scan/photo of the
  original.

## Photo d'identité

- Must be a **photo d'identité agréée** (norm ISO/IEC 19794-5): **recent (< 6 months)**,
  **35 × 45 mm**, plain light background (**not white**), head bare and centred, neutral
  expression, mouth closed, eyes open. Taken by a **photographe habilité** or a **cabine
  agréée** — selfies and home prints are rejected.
- The **ePhoto / e-photo code** (an agréé booth/photographer issues a digital code entered
  online) is the norm for the **permis de conduire** flow; for **CNI/passeport** the standard
  route is a compliant **physical** photo from a photographe habilité or cabine agréée. Either
  way it must be agréée.
- Don't just say "find an agréé booth" — help locate one. If the person's city or
  postal code is already known, a locator link with it pre-filled helps, e.g.
  `https://www.photomaton.com/fr-fr/trouver-un-photomaton/s?q=<address, URL-encoded>`.
  Photomaton is one of several agréé chains, not the only one — offer it as a
  convenient starting point, not the exclusive option.

## Timbre fiscal (électronique) — amounts as of 2026

- Bought online at **timbres.impots.gouv.fr** (PDF/QR or a 16-digit SMS reference) or at a
  **bureau de tabac agréé**; valid 1 year, refundable within 18 months. The amount depends on
  the document and the applicant's age — confirm the current amount on that page.
- **Passeport** (métropole): adult **86 €**, minor 15–17 **42 €**, minor < 15 **17 €** (higher
  from abroad; reduced in Guyane/Mayotte).
- **CNI**: **free** when you present the old card; **25 €** when renewing a **lost or stolen**
  card (12,50 € en Guyane; exemption possible for précarité).

## RIB / IBAN

- Required whenever the démarche pays or reimburses the user: **CAF (APL), France Travail,
  l'Assurance retraite, remboursements Ameli**. A French **IBAN starts with FR**; the account
  must be in the applicant's name.

## État civil — acte de naissance

- Three forms: **copie intégrale** (full act + filiation + mentions marginales), **extrait
  avec filiation**, **extrait sans filiation**. First passport/CNI, mariage and nationalité
  usually need the copie intégrale or an extrait avec filiation, **and it must be recent
  (< 3 months)** when used as an identity/état-civil proof.
- **Where**: **free** from the **mairie of the place of birth** or online via
  **service-public.gouv.fr** — never a paid intermediary. **Born abroad** (French citizen) →
  the **Service central d'état civil (SCEC) de Nantes** (MEAE), via the service-public.gouv.fr
  téléservice.
- Many communes are **dématérialisées (COMEDEC)**: the mairie fetches the acte directly, so the
  user does **not** need to provide it for a passport/CNI. Check before asking for it.
- Foreign-language civil-status documents need a **traduction par un traducteur assermenté**,
  sometimes with apostille/légalisation.

## General

- A recency window ("< 6 mois", "< 1 an") is counted from the **date of the application or
  RDV**, not from when the document was downloaded.
- Expect to show **originals** at an in-person appointment even when a scan was uploaded.
- Never tell a person a free service costs money, and never send them to a paid look-alike to
  obtain an acte d'état civil or a timbre.
