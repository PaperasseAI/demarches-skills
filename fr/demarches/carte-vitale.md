# Assurance Maladie & carte Vitale

Health coverage in France (PUMa) is a **right based on work or stable residence** —
after **3 months of stable, regular residence** for non-workers. The carte Vitale is
the card that makes reimbursements automatic; until it arrives, paper feuilles de soins
work.

## Éligibilité / Qui est concerné
- Workers from the first job day; non-workers after 3 months of stable residence;
  students via the dedicated CPAM student path; non-EU nationals need a regular stay.

## Étapes
1. Request affiliation at the **CPAM** of the department — newcomers typically file the
   **Cerfa 15763 (demande d'ouverture des droits)** with pièces, or via
   etudiant-etranger.ameli.fr for foreign students.
2. Receive the **numéro de sécurité sociale provisoire**, then the **définitif**.
3. Create the **compte ameli**, order the **carte Vitale** (photo + ID upload).
4. Declare a **médecin traitant** to avoid reduced reimbursements; consider a
   **mutuelle** (complementary insurance) — the CPAM covers only the base part.

## Pièces justificatives
- ID/passport + titre de séjour or visa, **acte de naissance** (translated by a
  traducteur assermenté if foreign — see `../pieces/pieces-justificatives.md`),
  justificatif de domicile, RIB, proof of work or of 3 months' residence.

## Délais et échéances
- Expect weeks-to-months for the numéro définitif; the carte Vitale follows. Keep every
  feuille de soins for retroactive reimbursement.

## Portail / Où faire la démarche
- **ameli.fr** (compte ameli) and the local CPAM; foreign students:
  **etudiant-etranger.ameli.fr**. Free.

## Pièges connus
- Waiting for the carte Vitale to see a doctor — coverage exists before the card.
- Foreign birth certificates rejected for lack of a sworn translation.
- Skipping the médecin traitant declaration (lower reimbursement rate).
