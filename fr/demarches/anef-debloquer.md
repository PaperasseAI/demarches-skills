# ANEF bloqué — débloquer une demande qui ne s'enregistre pas

The ANEF téléservice (administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr) is the
**only** channel for most titre de séjour applications — and it fails often:
*« une erreur empêche l'enregistrement des informations saisies »* at submission,
uploads rejected, accounts stuck. Because you cannot deposit the demande, you get
**no récépissé** — so a blockage near your titre's expiry threatens your right to
stay, work and travel. Courts have recognised exactly this: the State must fix the
téléservice's dysfunctions and provide an **alternative channel** when it fails
(CE, 3 juin 2026-checked ruling of 2022, n° 452798 e.a.), and juges des référés
have ordered préfectures to register blocked demandes (e.g. TA Versailles, ord.
14/04/2026).

## Étape 0 — try the known field-level causes first (observed practice, 2026-06,
from ANTS support's own replies)
The generic "check your input" advice hides **specific undocumented rules**:
- Avoid the characters **& * $ ( ) ° : ; , _ " /** anywhere in the form.
- **« Ville » field**: digits are blocking — if an **arrondissement number** was
  auto-filled (e.g. "Paris 8"), delete it and keep only the city name.
- **Phone numbers**: enter **without spaces**.
- **Naturalisation**: employer / host **name fields** accept only letters and
  "-" — no digits or other characters.
- **Marriage declaration**: the place of marriage takes **max 21 characters** —
  city name only.
- **CST « entrepreneur / profession libérale » (avis sur projet)**: if a first
  attempt bugged, redepose the demande **with a different email address** than
  the first attempt.
- Compress attachments to the size limits; retry from a **computer**, not mobile.

## Étape 1 — the ANEF/ANTS support form, with the evidence they will demand
Contact support via the **ANEF portal's own contact form** —
`administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr/particuliers/#/contact` (a
reference number « Ref… » tracks the case). Note: this is the ÉTRANGERS form; the
generic `ants.gouv.fr → contact` routes to the passport/carte-grise queue instead.
The phone line **0806 001 620** (ANTS/France Titres CCC) is the same team. To avoid
a round-trip, supply their evidence spec immediately (observed practice, 2026-06):
- **Full-run screenshots**: every step of the saisie **including the first**, up
  to the error — each with **date, time and URL visible**, taken **from a
  computer**;
- a copy of your **current titre**, your **postal address**, **email**, **phone**.
You can also call the **ANEF Centre de contacts citoyens (CCC)**: **0806 001 620**
(tarif appel local, non surtaxé), Mon–Fri — hours vary by period (commonly ~8h30–17h;
some regions wider), confirm on ants.gouv.fr. The CCC handles titre-de-séjour
technical difficulties and can advise on the bug.
Expect a first generic reply (the checklist above), then a technical escalation.
A real escalation exists: cases get taken over by the **DGEF** (Direction
générale des étrangers en France), typically with a **written response deadline**
("sous 15 jours") — keep that commitment: it matters later.

## Étape 2 — when support stalls: escalate on the record
- **Relance** on the same reference once any stated deadline lapses — cite the
  lapsed commitment and request either unblocking **or an alternative deposit
  channel** (the CE-mandated fallback).
- **Saisine du Défenseur des droits** (defenseurdesdroits.fr — free, no avocat
  needed): practitioners report it is the most effective pre-litigation lever;
  it formally interpellates the préfecture and typically forces a response in
  **4–8 weeks**. Your exposé des faits = the dated trail (first error, support
  reference, screenshots sent, lapsed deadlines).
- **Préfecture RDV / guichet** where one exists for blocked téléservice cases.

## Étape 3 — the legal rung (avocat en droit des étrangers)
- **Référé « mesures utiles »** (art. L.521-3 CJA): the juge des référés orders
  the préfecture/ANEF support to register or unblock the demande, **sous
  astreinte**. Courts accept that the **technical blockage itself establishes
  the urgency**, especially for a renouvellement near expiry.
- Find and verify counsel on **avocat.fr** (CNB annuaire, spécialité droit des
  étrangers); the **ADDE** association groups specialised avocats; **Cimade /
  GISTI permanences** give free orientation.

## Pièces justificatives
- The screenshot set (full run, date/time/URL visible, computer), current titre,
  the support reference number(s) and every reply — the dated trail IS the case.

## Délais et échéances
- Start at the **first blocking error**, not near expiry: each support round trip
  takes days-to-weeks, DGEF commitments run ~15 days, a DdD saisine 4–8 weeks.
- A blocked **dépôt** means **no récépissé** — the expiry of the current titre is
  the real clock; don't let support cycles consume it.

## Portail / Où faire la démarche
- ANEF: administration-etrangers-en-france.interieur.gouv.fr · support via the
  official ANEF/ANTS contact form · Défenseur des droits: defenseurdesdroits.fr.
- Paid facilitators exist and can carry the correspondence burden — they are
  **private services** (no legal monopoly, no privileged ANEF access); judge them
  by transparency (clear pricing, no pretend-official branding). For contentieux,
  only an **avocat** can act.

## Pièges connus
- Retrying blindly for weeks instead of capturing the **evidence spec** at the
  first error (support restarts the clock by re-asking for screenshots).
- Screenshots without **date/time/URL** or missing the **first step** — rejected,
  another round trip lost.
- Waiting out "ne nous recontactez pas" **after** the stated deadline has lapsed —
  once it lapses, relance on the record.
- Confusing the ANEF (interieur.gouv.fr) with paid look-alike sites.
