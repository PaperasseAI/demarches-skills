# Aide au logement (APL / ALS)

The CAF pays a monthly housing benefit to tenants who qualify — **students and
foreigners with legal stay included**. Rights start the month **after** the request:
apply as soon as the lease starts, the benefit is not retroactive.

## Éligibilité / Qui est concerné
- Tenant (or colocataire) of a decent dwelling that is the main residence, landlord not
  a close family member; means-tested on the last 12 months' income (updated quarterly).
- Foreign nationals need a **valid right to stay**; students in CROUS or private housing
  qualify like anyone else.

## Étapes
1. Run the **simulator** on caf.fr to estimate the right.
2. Create the CAF account and file "Demande d'aide au logement" online.
3. Provide the landlord/lease details (the CAF often confirms with the landlord) and RIB.
4. Track "Mon Compte" — respond quickly to pièces requests; payment usually starts the
   month after the demande, on the 5th.

## Pièces justificatives
- RIB in your name, lease + landlord details, income of the reference period, titre de
  séjour for non-EU nationals, student card / enrolment if applicable.

## Délais et échéances
- **No retroactivity**: rights open the month following the request — file at move-in.
- Déclare quarterly income updates when requested (automatic for most since the
  contemporaneous-income reform).

## Portail / Où faire la démarche
- **caf.fr** → Mon Compte → Mes démarches. Free. (Agricultural regime: msa.fr.)

## Pièges connus
- Waiting "until settled" and losing the first months.
- Colocation: each colocataire files their own demande with their share of the rent.
- The benefit is suspended if the CAF learns the dwelling isn't the main residence.
